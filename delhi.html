New Delhi.

A city of grand avenues, ancient empires, and modern evolution.

Famous Places to Go

From architectural marvels of the Mughal era to the sweeping boulevards of Lutyens' Delhi.

Heritage

Qutub Minar

A 73-meter tall brick minaret built in 1193. This UNESCO site is a victory tower representing the arrival of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Architecture

Lotus Temple

The Baháʼí House of Worship, notable for its flower-like shape. A serene haven designed to welcome people of all faiths.

UNESCO Site

Humayun’s Tomb

The first garden-tomb on the Indian subcontinent, it inspired several major architectural innovations, culminating in the Taj Mahal.

National Landmark

India Gate

A majestic war memorial standing at the heart of the city. At night, Kartavya Path glows with the spirit of the nation.

Iconic Places to Eat

Delve into a culinary landscape that spans from centuries-old street stalls to world-class modern Indian dining.

Fine Dining

Indian Accent

Award-winning modern Indian gastronomy by Chef Manish Mehrotra. Located in The Lodhi, it reinterprets nostalgic flavors with global flair.

Heritage Street Food

Paranthe Wali Gali

The legendary narrow lane in Chandni Chowk where generations have served deep-fried stuffed paranthas since the 1870s.

Mughlai Legend

Karim’s

Established in 1913 near Jama Masjid, it remains the ultimate destination for authentic Mughal royalty-style kebabs and stews.

Fine Dining

Bukhara

A global icon at ITC Maurya. Famous for its Dal Bukhara and rustic North-West Frontier cuisine cooked in traditional clay ovens.
